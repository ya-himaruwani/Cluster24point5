Theory Note - Lesson 1: Algebra

Algebra is a branch of mathematics that deals with symbols and the rules for manipulating those symbols. In Lesson 1, we will introduce the fundamental concepts of algebra:

  1. Variables and Constants: In algebra, we use letters to represent variables and fixed numbers as constants. Variables can take various values, and constants remain unchanged.
  2. Expressions: Algebraic expressions are combinations of variables, constants, and operators (like +, -, *, /). They represent mathematical phrases without an equals sign.
  3. Equations: Equations involve an equality sign (=) and express a relationship between two algebraic expressions. Solving equations helps find the values of variables that make the equation true.
  4. Polynomials: Polynomials are expressions with one or more terms. They play a crucial role in algebra and are classified based on the number of terms they contain.
  5. Factoring: Factoring involves expressing a polynomial as the product of its factors. This process is essential for simplifying expressions and solving equations.
  6. Linear Equations and Inequalities: We will explore linear equations and inequalities, which involve the first power of variables. Solving them is a foundational skill in algebra.

By the end of Lesson 1, you will have a solid understanding of these algebraic concepts, setting the stage for more advanced topics in subsequent lessons.
